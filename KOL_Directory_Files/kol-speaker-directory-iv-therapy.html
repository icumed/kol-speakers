    Biography:

    Joan Hebden RN, MS, CIC received her baccalaureate and master's degrees from the University of Maryland School of Nursing. Her clinical background includes general medicine, oncology and cardiothoracic intensive care. She is currently employed by Wolters Kluwer Health Clinical Solutions as a program manager for Sentri7, the company's infection prevention surveillance application and as an infection prevention consultant. She served as the Director of Infection Prevention and Control for 28 years at the University of Maryland Medical Center in Baltimore, Maryland.

    An accomplished practitioner, Ms. Hebden has presented at national epidemiology conferences, participated in research regarding the transmission of multi-drug resistant bacteria, contributed chapters on infection control to nursing resource texts, and published in medical and infection control journals.     A Synopsis of Joan's Message(s):

    Joan defines the frequency of catheter occlusions and makes the association with catheter colonization and infection. She defines the product evaluation process, discusses the process of bringing new technologies into a facility's practice, and finally how to make the clinical and business case for a new product, using Neutron as the example new technology.

    Past Positions:

    • Director of Nursing, Infusion Care, Inc.
    • Nutrition Support Nurse Specialist, Santa Clara Medical Center
    • Head Nurse, Special care Unit, Mercy Hospital, Miami, FL
    • Head Nurse, Cardiovascular / Thoracic, Jackson Memorial Hospital, Miami, FL

    Professional Focus/Area of Expertise:

    Dr. Ryder is a nationally recognized expert in medical device-related biofilm infections and the use and management of vascular access devices.

    Curriculum Vitae:

    Download a copy of Marcia Ryder's CV.

    Biography:

    Marcia Ryder’s extensive experience in nursing includes positions as Clinical Director of a Special Care Unit and a Cardiovascular/Thoracic Unit, Clinical Nurse Specialist in Nutrition Support, and Director of Nursing in Home Infusion. Dr. Ryder received her nursing diploma from the Western Pennsylvania Hospital School of Nursing in Pittsburgh, Pa. and her Bachelor’s, Master’s, and Doctoral degrees from the University of California, San Francisco. Her dissertation research investigated the effect of pulsed electrical fields on biofilm bacteria.

    After many years as an independent consultant, researcher and educator, Dr. Ryder is currently the Research Coordinator of the USC Center for Medical Biofilm Research at the University of Southern California, Los Angeles, CA. She is a nationally recognized expert in the use and management of vascular access devices; served as president of the Association for Vascular Access (AVA) and now serves as co-chair of APIC’s Research Foundation’s Scientific Research Council and member of the Editorial Advisory Board for APIC’s publication “Prevention Strategist”. Dr. Ryder was honored last year as the recipient of the “2007 Educator of the Year” awarded by Infection Control Today.

    A Synopsis of Marcia's Message(s):

    Dr. Ryder explains the significance of biofilm formation in needle-free devices; analyzes and compares the design features of common needle-free devices; and highlights intervention to prevent catheter-related bloodstream (CR-BSI) infections related to needle-free devices.

    Her talk discusses the pathogenesis of bloodstream infections, biofilm formation and lifecycle, critical needle-free connector design considerations, and the results of ongoing needle-free connector biofilm research.

    Recent Publications:

    Comparison of Bacterial Transfer and Biofilm Formation on Intraluminal Catheter Catheter Surfaces Among Eight Connectors in a Clinically Simulated in vitro Model.

    Differences in Bacterial Transfer and Fluid Path Colonization through Needlefree Connector-Catheter Systems In Vitro.

    Bacterial transfer through needlefree connectors: Comparison of nine different devices.

    2007 Educator of the Year, Infection Control Today.

    Needleless Devices...how device design effects the development of biofilm.
